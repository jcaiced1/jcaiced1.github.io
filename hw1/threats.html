Main attacks

  • Phishing: fake emails or pages that try to collect credentials.
  • Malware: software used to spy, damage files, or open backdoors.
  • Ransomware: attackers encrypt files and demand money.

Why it works

Most attacks start with social engineering, not technical complexity. Attackers usually exploit urgency, fear, or curiosity to push people into quick decisions before they verify a message.

Simple example

A fake message says your account expires today and asks you to click a link immediately. If the user reacts under pressure, they may submit credentials on a malicious page that looks legitimate.
